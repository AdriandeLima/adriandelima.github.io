They performed Me Llaman la primorosa from Rossini’s El Barbero de Sevilla, and Faure’s Les Roses d’Ispahan. The recording session took place in the Elgar Concert Hall and ran for 40:44. It was recorded on a Zoom F8, using an LCR mic setup for the piano, a stage A/B pair for reverb, and an A/B pair and spot mic for the vocals. Ultimately, I decided to not use the vocal A/B pair, as I thought the stereo pair didn’t fit as well within the frequency spectrum of the piano as the spot mic did. Me Llaman la primorosa Les Roses d’Ispahan Further Editing Details Mix Session I used two REAPER sessions to edit the recording, a MIX session for mixing the recordings, and adjusting the levels and EQs of individual mics. I then imported this as a sub-project into an EDIT session where the various takes where spliced together to fix any performance errors. This is the mixer of the MIX session. All the individual tracks are included in track folders to allow easier editing of EQs for microphone pairs. Note the VOCAL AB wasn’t used in the end, so it’s muted. Each track is hard panned to their respective directions. Most of the levels are left at their default values, with a slight reduction in the STAGE AB volume, and the CENTER channel of the PIANO LCR, to allow space for the VOCAL SPOT in the centre of the mix. Markers were inserted in the MIX session, indicating the various take positions. These were automatically embedded in the sub-project file in the EDIT session to make cutting the takes easier. An EQ with an HP filter was applied to the STAGE AB folder at about 250 Hz to reduce the muddiness of the reverb. The top end is also cut slightly to reduce harshness when stacked with the other mic pairs. Again, a small HP filter is applied to the PIANO LCR and VOCAL SPOT to reduce the muddiness of the piano, in both the piano microphones, and the audio bleed in the vocal microphone. The VOCAL SPOT was placed slightly too close to the singer, and as such picked up quite a lot of mouth noise and clicks and had some harsh plosives and breaths. To reduce this, I briefly reduce the volume of the vocal spot during rests, as pictured. Below is a table of the edits in the MIX session where I reduced the vocal spot’s volume. Edit number Start time in source 1 08:50.893 2 08:54.746 3 19:52.103 4 20:08.931 5 22:39.572 6 23:34.061 7 27:06.439 8 27:58.443 9 28:15.221 10 28:48.485 11 35:15.410 12 36:37.670 13 38:18.701 14 38:25.373 15 39:08.744 16 39:40.876 A small amount of compression was also added to the VOCAL SPOT to reduce the level of the louder sections, especially in Me Llaman la primorosa. I used ReaComp compressor and found a threshold of -17.6dB and a ratio of 5.59:1 worked quite well, without over compressing the vocals. Beginning at 15.16, 33.22 and 38.49 in the MIX session I also periodically lowered the volume of the PIANO LCR slightly, to better balance it with the voice. Edit Session Importing the MIX session into the EDIT session as a sub-project renders the MIX session down to a single track, which allows much easier editing and keeping track of takes. Both pieces were edited on their own tracks, with their own REVERB buses to allow both pieces to have different reverb settings. The splices happen mostly right before the beginning of a note, with very small crossfades to mask the edit point. A big challenge with this edit was working around large vocal phrases, and piano pedal changes. The crossfades are miniscule, and the cuts happen just before a spike in the waveform to mask the edit points. The volume levels are left at their default values, because all the mixing has been done in the MIX session. The levels of the REVERB buses are adjusted respectively. For Me Llaman la primorosa I used two different EQs, before and after the artificial reverb, to adjust the sound of the reverb track. I boosted the lows and cut the highs before the reverb, both before and after. I decided to use a smaller amount of artificial reverb to due to the speed of the piece, in order to keep the clarity of the music. I used an impulse response called 1 Halls 03 Small Hall M-to-S.wav, with a decay time of 2.5s, and applied -18dB of gain inside of ReaVerb. For Les Roses d’Ispahan I wanted to go for a more ethereal sound, so I used a long 3.9s impulse response called 1 Halls 10 Concert Hall M-to-S.wav, again with -18dB of gain, but the level of the reverb is higher in the final mix. I used an EQ to cut some of the high end before the reverb, so as to keep the clarity of the vocal material in the mix, without too much reverb on it. A slight fade was applied to the beginning and end of each piece, to avoid any accidental pops/clicks. A small fade was applied to the beginning and end of each piece to avoid any clicks/pops Finally, I rendered the piece to a single WAV file, using Time selection bounds, 48khz Sample rate and 24bit PCM bit depth.
