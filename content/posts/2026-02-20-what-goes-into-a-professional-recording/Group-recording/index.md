Recording Session Information
The recording session consisted of a singer (Jess Baldry) and piano (Toby Baker) ensemble.
They performed Me Llaman la primorosa from Rossini’s El Barbero de Sevilla, and Faure’s
Les Roses d’Ispahan. The recording session took place in the Elgar Concert Hall and ran for
40:44. It was recorded on a Zoom F8, using an LCR mic setup for the piano, a stage A/B pair
for reverb, and an A/B pair and spot mic for the vocals. Ultimately, I decided to not use the
vocal A/B pair, as I thought the stereo pair didn’t fit as well within the frequency spectrum
of the piano as the spot mic did. The recording was edited in REAPER.

Further Editing Details
Mix Session
I used two REAPER sessions to edit the recording, a MIX session for mixing the recordings,
and adjusting the levels and EQs of individual mics. I then imported this as a sub-project into
an EDIT session where the various takes where spliced together to fix any performance
errors.
This is the mixer of the MIX session. All the individual tracks are included in track folders to
allow easier editing of EQs for microphone pairs. Note the VOCAL AB wasn’t used in the end,
so it’s muted. Each track is hard panned to their respective directions. Most of the levels are
left at their default values, with a slight reduction in the STAGE AB volume, and the CENTER
channel of the PIANO LCR, to allow space for the VOCAL SPOT in the centre of the mix.
Markers were inserted in the MIX session, indicating the various take positions. These were
automatically embedded in the sub-project file in the EDIT session to make cutting the takes
easier.
An EQ with an HP filter was applied to the STAGE AB folder at about 250 Hz to reduce the
muddiness of the reverb. The top end is also cut slightly to reduce harshness when stacked
with the other mic pairs.
Again, a small HP filter is applied to the PIANO LCR and VOCAL SPOT to reduce the
muddiness of the piano, in both the piano microphones, and the audio bleed in the vocal
microphone.

A small amount of compression was also added to the VOCAL SPOT to reduce the level of

I used ReaComp compressor and found a threshold of -17.6dB and a ratio of 5.59:1 worked

Edit Session
Importing the MIX session into the EDIT session as a sub-project renders the MIX session
down to a single track, which allows much easier editing and keeping track of takes.
Both pieces were edited on their own tracks, with their own REVERB buses to allow both
pieces to have different reverb settings. The splices happen mostly right before the
beginning of a note, with very small crossfades to mask the edit point. A big challenge with
this edit was working around large vocal phrases, and piano pedal changes.
The crossfades are
miniscule, and the cuts
happen just before a
spike in the waveform to
mask the edit points.
The volume levels are left at their default values, because all the mixing has
been done in the MIX session. The levels of the REVERB buses are adjusted
respectively.
For Me Llaman la primorosa I used two different EQs, before and after the artificial reverb,
to adjust the sound of the reverb track. I boosted the lows and cut the highs before the
reverb, both before and after.
I decided to use a smaller amount of artificial reverb to due to the speed of the piece, in
order to keep the clarity of the music. I used an impulse response called 1 Halls 03 Small Hall
M-to-S.wav, with a decay time of 2.5s, and applied -18dB of gain inside of ReaVerb.
For Les Roses d’Ispahan I wanted to go for a more ethereal sound, so I used a long 3.9s
impulse response called 1 Halls 10 Concert Hall M-to-S.wav, again with -18dB of gain, but
the level of the reverb is higher in the final mix. I used an EQ to cut some of the high end
before the reverb, so as to keep the clarity of the vocal material in the mix, without too
much reverb on it.
A slight fade was applied to the beginning and end of each piece, to avoid any accidental
pops/clicks.
